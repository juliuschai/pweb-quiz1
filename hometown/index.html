Hometown

Jakarta


source

Born and raised in Jakarta has its pros and cons. One of the drawbacks is the horrendous traffic, to drive anywhere during rush hour not only wastes your time, but it also halves your lifespan. The constant switch between stopping and going doesn't allow a lapse in your concentration, other vehicles add to the suffering by not following road etiquette, and you're never going to have peace of mind with all the honking going on around you.

All that said, there's the existence of Trans Jakarta. The government provides accessible and affordable public transport to decrease traffic congestion. Costing only 3500 Rupiah, if your destination is within walking distance to the nearest Trans Jakarta station, you could save up a lot on transport fees, also avoiding the stress of driving in compact traffic along the way.

Having public transport should theoretically be a great fix. Well, it's not. Remember, nobody follows road etiquette. You're going to find a lot of motorcycles (and sometimes cars) in the bus lane. Not only that, based on my personal experience, there's a main road called Jl. Daan Mogot, where a part of the road is a single laned bridge. There's no bus lane on that bridge because it's a single laned bridge and corridor 3 buses will always go on that bridge. That part has been a bottleneck for YEARS and will continue being a bottleneck for years to come.
